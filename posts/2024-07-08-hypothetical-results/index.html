In research, don’t do things you don’t see the point of

research questions
Author

Jan Vanhove

Published

August 7, 2024

My overarching goal when teaching subject-matter classes is to help the students become more independent readers of quantitative research articles. This encompasses their getting more skilled at making sense of research articles without my help. But it also encompasses their becoming more proficient at drawing conclusions from research articles without relying on what the authors write in the discussion section. That is, they should ideally be able to read the introduction, methods, and results sections, and then answer the article’s research questions themselves.

Not easy. May not know what to look for in results section. (Often too much information.) To help them see forest for the trees: exercise. The idea is as follows:

  • Read introductory chapters (up to but not including results).
  • Draw a bunch graphs showing possible results, in stylised form.
  • Interpret each graph in term of research questions / hypotheses.
  • Also think about possible alternative explanations for results.
  • In doing so, figure out what kind of data patterns are relevant for answering the research questions.
  • In the results section, …
